Biography

1963

From his first experiences in Rio Grande do Norte in 1963, when he taught 300 adults to read and write in 45 days, Paulo Freire developed an innovative method of literacy, first adopted in Pernambuco. In response to the effective results, the Brazilian government (which, under President João Goulart, committed itself to basic reforms) approved the multiplication of these first experiences in a National Literacy Plan, which provided for the formation of mass educators and Rapid deployment of 20 thousand nuclei (the "cultural circles") throughout the country.

1964-1985

His career in Brazil was interrupted by the military coup of March 31, 1964. Accused of subversion, he spent 72 days in prison and then went into exile. In Chile, he worked for five years at the Chilean Institute for Agrarian Reform (ICIRA). At that time, he wrote his main book: Pedagogy of the Oppressed (1968).

1969-1971

In 1969, he taught at Harvard University (United States), and in the 1970s he was a consultant to the World Council of Churches (WCC) in Geneva, Switzerland. During this period, he provided educational advice to governments in poor countries, most of them on the African continent, which at the time lived a process of independence. 1969-1971

In 1969, he taught at Harvard University (United States), and in the 1970s he was a consultant to the World Council of Churches (WCC) in Geneva, Switzerland. During this period, he provided educational advice to governments in poor countries, most of them on the African continent, which at the time lived a process of independence. PARAGRAFO At the end of 1971, Freire made his first visit to Zambia and Tanzania. It then had a more significant participation in the education of Guinea-Bissau, Cape Verde, São Tomé and Príncipe. It also influenced the experiences of Angola and Mozambique.

1980-1990

In 1980, after 16 years of exile, he returned to Brazil, where he wrote two books considered as fundamental in his work: Pedagogy of Hope (1992) and À Sombra de Mangueira (1995). He taught at the State University of Campinas (Unicamp) and at the Pontifical Catholic University of São Paulo (PUC-SP). In 1989, she was Secretary of Education in the Municipality of São Paulo, under the city council of Luíza Erundina.
